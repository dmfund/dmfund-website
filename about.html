About Davis Macleod

A family investment office with a singular focus: backing exceptional entrepreneurs who acquire and build enduring businesses.

DM

Principal

Davis Macleod

Davis Macleod is a search fund investor and family office principal with over fifteen years of experience acquiring, operating, and growing small businesses across North America.

After an early career in finance and operations, Davis recognized the extraordinary potential of the search fund model — a path that empowers talented entrepreneurs to find, acquire, and lead an existing business. He founded this firm to provide patient, permanent capital alongside the strategic guidance and mentorship that first-time CEOs need most.

Davis takes an active role in every investment, serving on boards, mentoring management teams, and connecting his portfolio companies with a broad network of operators, advisors, and industry experts. His approach is grounded in the belief that the best returns come from investing in people and taking the long view.

Outside of investing, Davis is engaged in the broader search fund and entrepreneurship-through-acquisition community, contributing to research, mentoring aspiring searchers, and participating in industry conferences.

Search Fund 101

What is a Search Fund?

A search fund is an investment vehicle through which an aspiring entrepreneur (the "searcher") raises capital from investors to search for, acquire, and operate a privately held company.

Originated at Stanford GSB in the 1980s, the search fund model has become one of the most reliable paths to entrepreneurship through acquisition. Searchers are typically MBA graduates or experienced professionals who want to run a company from day one.

As search fund investors, we provide capital at two stages: first, to fund the search itself, and second, to fund the acquisition. Beyond capital, we serve as mentors, board members, and strategic partners throughout the searcher's journey.

The Search Fund Lifecycle

1. Raise Search Capital

Searcher raises a small fund ($400K-$600K) from investors to support a 2-year search for the right business.

2. Search & Identify

Full-time search to find a company meeting specific criteria: strong cash flows, defensible position, and growth potential.

3. Acquire

Searcher leads the acquisition, raising additional capital from their investor group to fund the purchase.

4. Operate & Grow

Searcher becomes CEO and runs the company with support from their board and investor group. Hold period is typically 5-10+ years.
